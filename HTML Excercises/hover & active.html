COSMOLOGY

Cosmology (from Ancient Greek is a branch of physics and metaphysics dealing with the nature of the universe. The term cosmology was first used in English in 1656 in Thomas Blount's Glossographia, and in 1731 taken up in Latin by German philosopher Christian Wolff, in Cosmologia Generalis. Religious or mythological cosmology is a body of beliefs based on mythological, religious, and esoteric literature and traditions of creation myths and eschatology. In the science of astronomy, cosmology is concerned with the study of the chronology of the universe.

ASTROPHYSICS

Astrophysics is a science that employs the methods and principles of physics and chemistry in the study of astronomical objects and phenomena.
